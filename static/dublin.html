Key Attractions

Dublin Castle

Location: Dame St, Dublin 2

Opening Hours: 9:45am - 5:45pm (Mon-Sat), 12:00pm - 5:45pm (Sun)

Admission: €12 per person

Dublin Castle has served as a military fortress, a prison, treasury, courts of law and the seat of English Administration in Ireland for 700 years. Today, it houses government offices and state rooms. The castle gardens are particularly beautiful and offer a peaceful retreat from the busy city.

Trinity College & Book of Kells

Location: College Green, Dublin 2

Opening Hours: 9:30am - 5:00pm (Mon-Sat), 12:00pm - 4:30pm (Sun)

Admission: €18 per person

Trinity College is Ireland's oldest university, founded in 1592. The highlight for many visitors is the Old Library and the Book of Kells exhibition. The Book of Kells is an illuminated manuscript Gospel book, created by Celtic monks around 800 AD. The Long Room, in the Old Library, is one of the most impressive libraries in the world and houses 200,000 of Trinity's oldest books.

Howth Cliff Path

Location: Howth Peninsula, Co. Dublin

Best Time to Visit: Morning or evening for best light and fewer crowds

Admission: Free

The Howth Cliff Path offers one of the most scenic walks near Dublin. The full loop is about 10km, but you can do shorter sections. The path provides spectacular views over Dublin Bay, Ireland's Eye, and on clear days, the Wicklow Mountains. The area is rich in wildlife, including seabirds, and in May you might see wildflowers blooming along the path. Sunset at Howth Head is particularly magical, making it a perfect spot for special moments.

National Botanic Gardens

Location: Glasnevin, Dublin 9

Opening Hours: 9:00am - 5:00pm daily

Admission: Free

The National Botanic Gardens are a green oasis just 3km from Dublin city center. The gardens contain over 15,000 plant species from around the world, including rare and endangered species. The Victorian glasshouses are architectural gems, particularly the Great Palm House and the Curvilinear Range. In May, many plants will be in bloom, making it a particularly beautiful time to visit.

Phoenix Park

Location: Phoenix Park, Dublin 8

Opening Hours: 24/7 (Visitor Centre: 9:30am - 5:30pm)

Admission: Free

Phoenix Park is one of the largest enclosed public parks in any European capital city. It's home to a herd of wild fallow deer, the Dublin Zoo, and the Victorian People's Flower Gardens. The park offers extensive walking and cycling paths through woodlands and open spaces. It's also home to Áras an Uachtaráin, the residence of the President of Ireland.

The Craic Den Comedy Club

Location: The Workmans Club, Dublin City

Shows: 7 nights a week

Admission: €15 per person

The Craic Den Comedy Club is where Ireland's best comedians take to the stage. With live club shows 7 nights a week in the heart of Dublin City, it's the perfect place to experience Irish humor and have a laugh-filled evening.

Travel Tips

Weather in May

Dublin in May typically has mild temperatures ranging from 7°C to 16°C (45°F to 61°F). While it's generally pleasant, be prepared for changeable conditions and occasional rain showers. Pack layers, a light raincoat, and comfortable waterproof walking shoes.

Currency & Money

Ireland uses the Euro (€). Credit and debit cards are widely accepted, but it's good to carry some cash for small purchases. ATMs are readily available throughout the city. Inform your bank of your travel plans to avoid any issues with your cards.

Tipping

Tipping is not as customary in Ireland as in some countries, but it is appreciated for good service. In restaurants, 10-15% is standard if service is not included. Taxi drivers and tour guides typically receive 10%. Bartenders are not usually tipped for drinks, but rounding up the bill is common.

Dining

For popular restaurants, especially on weekends, it's advisable to book in advance. Many pubs serve excellent food, often at better value than restaurants. Traditional Irish dishes to try include Irish stew, boxty (potato pancake), coddle (Dublin sausage and bacon stew), and seafood chowder.

Pub Etiquette

In Irish pubs, you typically order and pay at the bar rather than waiting for table service. If someone buys you a drink, it's customary to buy a round in return. "Cheers" or "Sláinte" (pronounced slawn-cha) is the traditional toast.

Special Proposal Tips

For the special moment at Howth Head, arrive at least 30 minutes before sunset to find the perfect spot. The viewpoint near the Nose of Howth or the area near the Baily Lighthouse offers the most spectacular views. If the weather is poor, consider the alternative locations mentioned in the itinerary.

Transportation Information

Getting from the Airport

Dublin Airport is located approximately 10km north of the city center. The most convenient options to reach the city are:

  • Aircoach: €16 per person one-way, runs 24/7, stops at major hotels and areas
  • Dublin Bus (Airlink): €7 per person one-way, frequent service to the city center
  • Taxi: Approximately €25-35 to the city center, convenient but more expensive

Public Transportation

Dublin has an extensive public transportation network:

  • Leap Visitor Card: €40 for 72 hours, covers all public transport including buses, DART, and trams
  • Dublin Bus: Extensive network covering the entire city
  • DART (Dublin Area Rapid Transit): Coastal train service, perfect for trips to Howth
  • Luas: Tram system with two lines (Red and Green) crossing the city

Getting to Howth

The DART train is the best way to reach Howth. Trains run approximately every 15-20 minutes from the city center (Connolly Station or Tara Street Station) and take about 30 minutes. The Howth station is located right in the village, a short walk from the start of the cliff path.
